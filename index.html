⚖ PLAYER COMPARE vs
EPA — Expected Points Added — measures how much each play changed a team's expected score. Every down-and-distance has an expected point value (1st & 10 at midfield is worth more than 3rd & 15 at your own 5); EPA is the change in that value caused by a play. A 4-yard run on 3rd & 3 is a good play (converts, keeps the drive) while a 4-yard pass on 3rd & 12 is a bad one — yardage alone can't tell them apart, EPA can. Per-play EPA here grades a player's efficiency on their touches: positive = adding value above an average play, negative = below. DELTA uses it alongside raw production to spot players whose counting stats flatter or undersell them — high volume with negative EPA is a warning sign; positive EPA on small volume hints at more if the role grows.
RB floor: ≥140 rushes + YAC/carry ≥3.0 → EPA treated as neutral. WR/TE: ≥80 targets → floor 0.92. QB: ≥300 plays → floor 0.88.

About DELTA
Dynasty Efficiency & Long-Term Analysis
What is DELTA?

DELTA evaluates dynasty players over a 2–3 year window using what they've actually done — production history, age relative to their position's prime, opportunity depth, and contract situation. It deliberately ignores market prices as an input, so the model can find gaps between what a player is worth and what the market pays.

Why ignore the market?

Most dynasty tools are self-referential — they reflect what other managers think, which reflects what other managers think. DELTA starts from first principles. Market value appears in the app as a comparison point and yardstick, never as an input to the score or verdict. The gap between model value and market value is the signal.

Glossary

Quick reference for DELTA's terms:

  • DELTA Score (0–99) — the proprietary grade of demonstrated dynasty value: production history, age vs. positional prime, opportunity, contract. League median ≈ 60.
  • Market Value — consensus trade value (FantasyCalc): what the market pays. Used as a yardstick only, never a model input.
  • Model Value — DELTA's own asset price, built from projected production. The gap between Model and Market is the whole product.
  • Verdict (Buy / Sell / Hold) — how a player's model-vs-market gap compares to the rest of the league. Centered on the population, so "buy" means cheaper than the typical player relative to the model.
  • Proj PPG — DELTA's projected points per game for the upcoming season, at your league's scoring.
  • Start Profile — per-start outcomes across recent seasons: hit (startable week), elite (slate-breaking week), miss (hurt your lineup). Weekly dependability, not season totals.
  • Alpha / Workhorse Score — role size grade (targets/touches share): how much of the offense actually flows through the player.
  • System Score (0–100) — offensive environment quality: coordinator, scheme fit, role clarity. League median ≈ 60. Enters the projection as a small multiplier.
  • ⚠ OC change — a new coordinator: everything demonstrated came under the old scheme, so the model discounts until the new one shows in real games. Rookies are exempt (no prior scheme to lose).
  • Offense style factors — validated play-by-play tendencies (2022–25): pre-snap motion helps RB production, heavy 12-personnel squeezes WR3s, pass-lean identity (PROE) lifts WR rooms. Small (±2–4%), explained on each affected card.
  • EPA / YPRR — per-play efficiency (Expected Points Added) and yards per route run: quality of play independent of volume.
  • Ripple Effects — measured value shifts on incumbent players when teammates arrive or depart, calibrated on eight seasons of history.
  • Format sensitivity — how much a player's value shifts between standard / half / full PPR, from their reception volume vs. position average.
  • 5th-year option — first-round rookie deals carry a team option for a fifth season; shown as "+opt" until exercised.
  • † stale — a market value not refreshed in the latest fetch; excluded from graded records until it updates.
What the model can't see
  • Recent injuries or transactions not yet reflected in production data — check the data freshness badge.
  • How a new offensive coordinator will actually deploy a player. The ⚠ OC flag applies a discount, but the model can't predict scheme fit.
  • Any future performance. DELTA scores what a player has demonstrated, not what they might become.
  • Situational upside from teammate changes — if a player signed with a new team this offseason, the model scores their historical production and new system score, but hasn't seen them in that role yet.
Reading the verdicts

Strong Buy / Buy / Hold / Sell / Strong Sell reflect market pricing relative to DELTA's model — not absolute player quality. A Sell on an RB35 means the market is paying more than the model thinks the player is worth at that tier, not that the player is useless. The Read section in each player card explains the specific gap driving the verdict.
